# California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) / CPRA
## Cal. Civ. Code 1798.100 et seq.

### 1798.105 — Right to deletion

A consumer shall have the right to request that a business delete any personal
information about the consumer which the business has collected. Upon receipt
of a verifiable consumer request, the business shall delete and direct service
providers to delete the consumer's personal information.

Exceptions to deletion: (a) complete the transaction; (b) security;
(c) debugging; (d) free speech; (e) comply with legal obligation;
(f) scientific/statistical research in the public interest;
(g) internal uses reasonably aligned with expectations;
(h) otherwise complying with legal obligation.

### 1798.100 — Right to know

A consumer shall have the right to request that a business disclose the
categories and specific pieces of personal information the business has
collected about the consumer.

### 1798.121 — Right to limit use of sensitive personal information

A consumer shall have the right to direct a business that collects sensitive
personal information to limit its use of that information to that use which
is necessary to perform the services or provide the goods requested.

### Sensitive Personal Information (Cal. Civ. Code 1798.140(ae))

Includes: SSN, driver's license, financial account with credentials, precise
geolocation, race/ethnic origin, religious beliefs, union membership, contents
of mail/email/texts, genetic data, biometric data, health information,
sex life or sexual orientation.
